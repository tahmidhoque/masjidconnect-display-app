MasjidConnect — Demo Call Script

Mindset: you are diagnosing a problem, not selling a product. Ask more than you tell.
Target length: 45–60 min
Situation → Pain → Demo → Close
Always end with a concrete next step
1
OPENING — Warm Up & Set the Agenda
2–3 MIN
Say"Assalamu Alaikum — great to connect with you. I'm [name] from MasjidConnect. Before I jump into anything, would it be okay if I asked you a few questions about your masjid first? That way I can make sure whatever I show you is actually relevant to your situation rather than just walking through everything."
➜ If they say "sure" — great. If they say "actually can you just show me the product?" — do a 2 min overview first, then say "let me pause and check — does any of that feel relevant to what you're dealing with?"
Then"Perfect. So tell me a bit about [Masjid name] — how many people do you typically get for Jumu'ah, and how long have you been running the masjid?"
➜ This is small talk but it matters — it tells you scale, maturity, and how organised they are. Listen for clues about who actually runs things.
2
DISCOVERY — Understand Their World
12–15 MIN
Situation
Ask"How are you currently displaying prayer times and announcements — TV screens, printed sheets, a whiteboard?"
Follow up: "And who updates that — a dedicated admin, a volunteer rota, or is it more ad-hoc?"
Ask"How many screens are you working with right now — or are you starting from scratch?"
Follow up: "Are they all in the same building or spread across different areas?"
Ask"How often does your content need to change — daily, weekly, or is it mostly static?"
Ask"Do you use any software today to manage any of this, or is it all manual?"
Pain
Ask"What's the biggest headache with how things work today?"
If they hesitate: "A lot of masajid tell us it's either the manual effort to keep it updated, or the times being wrong — does either of those ring a bell?"
Ask"Has anything gone wrong before — wrong prayer times on screen, an announcement that was out of date, something that caused confusion for the jama'ah?"
➜ If they share a story, don't rush past it. These moments build your demo narrative.
Ask"How much time does your team spend each week just keeping the displays updated?"
Ask"If you could wave a magic wand, what would the ideal setup look like for you?"
Decision
Ask"Who else would be involved in a decision like this — a management committee, trustees, the imam?"
If committee: "Would it help if I could join a call with them, or put something together you can share?"
Ask"Is there a budget set aside for something like this, or is that still being figured out?"
If no budget: Don't panic — note it and come back to ROI in the close.
Ask"What's driving you to look at this now — is there a particular event coming up, or has something specific changed?"
Ask"What would a good outcome from today look like for you?"
➜ Sets their expectations and tells you how to close.
3
BRIDGE — Transition from Discovery to Demo
1 MIN
Say"Okay, that's really helpful — thank you. So it sounds like the main things for you are [X] and [Y]. Let me show you exactly how MasjidConnect handles those. I'll skip the parts that aren't relevant to you and focus on what you've told me matters most."
➜ Fill in [X] and [Y] from your notes. If they said "volunteers spend ages updating times" — say that back verbatim. It shows you listened and the demo feels personal even if it's the same every time.
4
DEMO — Show Don't Tell
20–25 MIN
A
The Display — Start Here (Emotional Hook)
Say"So this is what your congregation would actually see when they walk in. Prayer times, next iqamah countdown, announcements — all updating automatically."
Pause here. Let them take it in. Then: "Can you see that working in your masjid?"
B
Admin Portal — The 'Wow That's Easy' Moment
Say"You mentioned [volunteer name / admin] manages the content. Here's what they'd use — it's a web dashboard, no app to install. They can update an announcement from their phone in 30 seconds."
Show a live update if possible. Nothing beats watching the screen change in real time.
C
Prayer Times — Auto-Calculated
Say"You don't enter prayer times manually. You set your location and calculation method once, and it calculates them automatically — including adjusting for daylight saving. If Ramadan timetables change, you update once and every screen reflects it."
D
Emergency Alerts — High Impact, Always Resonates
Say"This one's important. If there's ever an urgent situation in the masjid — a medical emergency, a security concern, anything — you can push an alert to every screen instantly, from your phone. It overrides whatever is displaying. Most masajid haven't thought about this until they need it."
➜ Watch their reaction here. If they lean forward or say "oh that's really useful," anchor the whole close around it.
E
Offline Mode — The Reliability Guarantee
Say"One thing that causes problems for masajid using other solutions is internet outages — Jumu'ah is about to start and the screen goes blank. With MasjidConnect, prayer times are cached locally on the device. Even if the internet goes down completely, the display keeps running perfectly."
F
The Hardware — Raspberry Pi
Say"The device running this is a Raspberry Pi — it's a small computer about the size of a deck of cards. It plugs into your TV's HDMI port. Setup takes about 20 minutes. You don't need a technician. And if one ever breaks, you can replace it yourself for around £50."
Check in after each section: "Does that address the concern you had about [X]?"

When you get a hard question: acknowledge it → reframe → answer. Never get defensive.
Remember: silence after a question is fine
Never interrupt an objection mid-sentence
"That's a fair point" buys you thinking time
5
HANDLING OBJECTIONS
"We don't have the budget right now."
Say"That's totally fair — can I ask, what's the current setup costing you in volunteer hours and printed materials each month? Sometimes when we add that up, the numbers are closer than people expect. And I can also look at whether there's a phased option that might work."
"We need to discuss with the committee / trustees."
Say"Of course — that's exactly how it should work. Would it help if I joined that meeting, even just for 10 minutes to answer questions? Or I can put together a one-pager with the key points that makes it easy for you to present it."
➜ Always offer both options — some prefer you join, some prefer to own the room themselves.
"Can't we just use a TV slideshow or a free app?"
Say"You absolutely can, and a lot of masajid start there. The difference is: with those, someone still has to manually update prayer times, there's no emergency alert system, and times won't auto-adjust for daylight saving or Ramadan. MasjidConnect handles all of that automatically. But if the timing isn't right, there's no pressure."
"What happens if the internet goes down?"
Say"Great question — this is actually something we designed specifically for. Prayer times and your schedule are cached locally on the device, so the display keeps running normally even with no internet. It syncs back up automatically when the connection returns."
"What if the Raspberry Pi breaks?"
Say"It's standard, widely available hardware — a replacement costs around £50 and is available on Amazon next-day. All your content and settings are stored in the cloud, so you just plug in a new one and it restores itself. We can also walk you through it over a call."
"Is this secure? We don't want strangers controlling our screens."
Say"Completely understandable. Access is role-based — you control who can log into the admin portal and what they can change. Emergency alerts can be locked to specific admins only. The device communicates outbound to our servers; there's no open inbound access to your network."
"We're already looking at [competitor]."
Say"Good to know — what's standing out to you about them so far?"
➜ Stop talking and listen. Their answer tells you exactly what to address. Never attack a competitor by name — compare on specific features only.
"We're not ready right now — maybe in a few months."
Say"No problem at all. What would need to happen between now and then for this to make sense? I want to make sure that when you are ready, I can pick up exactly where we left off."
➜ Then schedule a specific follow-up date before you hang up. "Shall we put something in the diary for [month]?"
6
CLOSING — Always End With a Next Step
5 MIN
Before closing, summarise what they told you: "So based on what you've shared, [pain point] is the main thing you're trying to solve — and it sounds like [feature] is the part that would make the biggest difference for you."
If they're ready to move forward
Say"Brilliant. The next step would be [trial / order / setup call]. I'll send you everything you need within the hour — does that work?"
If they need committee approval
Say"Completely understand. I'll send you a summary you can share with them — and I'm happy to join the meeting if that helps. When are they next getting together?"
If they're unsure / need to think
Say"No rush at all. What would need to be true for this to be a clear yes for you? Is there something specific I can help clarify?"
If there's no budget yet
Say"Understood. When does your next budget discussion happen? I'd love to make sure you have the numbers to hand when that conversation comes up."
Universal close — use every time
Say"Before we wrap up — what's the single most useful thing I can send you after this call?"
➜ Then: "And let's put a date in the diary now to follow up — would [specific date] work?" Never end without a booked next step.
VS
COMPETITORS & YOUR DIFFERENTIATORS
IslamicFinder / MuslimPro
Consumer apps — not built for signage. No display mode, no admin portal, no emergency alerts.
Yodeck / Screenly / Rise
Generic signage — no prayer times, no iqamah countdowns, no masjid-specific features. Manual setup required for everything.
Volunteer-built solution
"What happens when that volunteer moves on or isn't available? Who maintains it then?"
Other masjid software
Ask: "Does it have an offline mode? Emergency alerts? Do you own the hardware or rent it?"
Why MasjidConnect wins
Built for masajid — not adapted from a church tool
Offline-first — prayer times never go blank
Emergency alerts to all screens in seconds
You own the hardware — no subscription lock-in
Auto DST & Ramadan adjustment — set it and forget it
£50 hardware — replaceable same-day by anyone
DO: Ask before presenting · Reflect their words back · Pause after questions · Name a next step
DON'T: Pitch before listening · Attack competitors · End without a date · Promise what you can't deliver
IF STUCK: "That's a fair point — can I ask what's driving that concern?" buys you time every time.
